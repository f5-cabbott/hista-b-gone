## System Architecture: Root Cause Mechanics

Sudden-onset histamine intolerance trailing tropical travel is rarely a primary immune defect; it is typically an induced architectural failure of the gut wall. When acute system dysregulation occurs post-travel, it is driven by two primary physiological triggers:

### 1. The Scombroid "Reset" (Acute Exogenous Overdose)
Ingestion of pelagic or reef fish (Mahi-Mahi, Tuna, Amberjack) that has undergone minor temperature abuse allows the bacterium *Morganella morganii* to rapidly convert native histidine into toxic levels of pure histamine. 
* This massive, acute payload acts as a **biological circuit breaker blow**. 
* The exogenous volume completely overwhelms native Diamine Oxidase (DAO) clearance capacity, forcing widespread, aggressive mast cell degranulation across the lamina propria. 
* The system locks into a chronic inflammatory feedback loop where localized mast cells remain hyper-sensitized long after the initial toxin has cleared.

### 2. Tropical Pathogen Dysbiosis (The DAO Wipeout)
Acute sub-clinical infection from coastal foodborne pathogens or localized gastroenteritis (*Vibrio*, *Salmonella*, *Campylobacter*) triggers profound localized inflammation.
* Native DAO enzymes are synthesized and secreted exclusively by the **microvilli (the brush border projections)** lining the enterocytes of the small intestine.
* Pathogen-induced inflammatory cascades physically shear and flatten these microvilli, taking the local DAO manufacturing factory entirely offline.
* Without this protective chemical shield, standard ambient dietary histamines cross the damaged barrier unhindered, causing systemic flushing, neurological redlining, and vascular volatility.

## The Amine Heist: Enzymatic Paralysis & Vascular Mimicry

Biogenic amines (tyramine, cadaverine, putrescine, spermidine) present in aged, fermented, or stored foods do not merely act as passive mast cell triggers. They systematically sabotage clearance biochemistry via two vectors:

```
[Biogenic Amines] 
   │
   ├──► Binds to DAO Enzymes ──► [Blocks Histamine Clearance] ──► Systemic Histamine Spike
   │
   └──► Direct Vascular Action ─► [Displaces Norepinephrine] ──► Vasoconstriction / Rebound Flushing
```

### 1. Competitive Inhibition (Enzymatic Paralysis)
Diamine Oxidase (DAO) lacks an exclusive lock-and-key fit for histamine; it is a broad-spectrum scavenger designed to clear all diamines. When a diet contains high levels of **putrescine** (citrus, stored plant matter) or **cadaverine** (aged proteins), these molecules saturate the active binding sites of the DAO enzyme. They crowd out histamine. While the remaining DAO hardware is occupied metabolizing alternative amines, ambient dietary histamine passes completely unchecked into systemic circulation.

### 2. Direct Tissue Mimicry (The Vascular Trap)
* **Tyramine Adrenaline Mimicry:** Found heavily in aged items and avocados, tyramine travels up sympathetic nerve endings and physically displaces **norepinephrine** from storage vesicles. This causes sudden acute vasoconstriction (spiking blood pressure) followed by rapid rebound vasodilation, mimicking a massive mast cell dump without utilizing immune pathways.
* **Barrier Corrosion:** High concentrations of putrescine and cadaverine directly corrode the mucosal lining, increasing tight junction permeability. This permits intact food antigens to breach the barrier, directly activating the underlying mast cell matrix.

## The 3-Phase Remediation Protocol

To break the inflammatory loop, the framework moves sequentially through three distinct operational phases.

### Phase 1: Defuse & Clear
*   **Operational Focus:** Lower ambient histamine and biogenic amine influx to zero to give existing native enzymes an absolute operational vacation.
*   **Duration:** 14 Days.
*   **Supplement Stack:**
    *   **Exogenous DAO Enzyme:** 1 capsule (e.g., *NaturDAO*) taken 20 minutes prior to every meal to manually clear dietary amines in the gut lumen.
    *   **H1 + H2 Receptor Blockers:** Standard *Cetirizine* or *Fexofenadine* coupled with *Famotidine* to physically occupy systemic receptors and mute symptom cascades.
*   **Dietary Baseline:** Zero leftovers. Cooking must utilize fresh-cooked or flash-frozen-at-sea animal proteins exclusively. No canned, aged, slow-cooked, or fermented items.

### Phase 2: Quench & Stabilize
*   **Operational Focus:** Lock down the cell membranes of hyper-reactive mast cells lining the GI tract and skin barrier to stop spontaneous degranulation.
*   **Duration:** 21 Days (overlapping transition out of Phase 1).
*   **Supplement Stack:**
    *   **Quercetin Phytosome:** 500mg taken 30 minutes prior to morning and evening meals on an empty stomach (lipid-bound form for maximum systemic absorption).
    *   **Luteolin:** 100mg daily to work synergistically with Quercetin to inhibit mast cell cytokine release.
    *   **Buffered Vitamin C:** Pure *Calcium* or *Magnesium Ascorbate* to catalyze the breakdown of circulating histamine without irritating raw mucosa.
*   **Dietary Baseline:** Introduction of low-histamine, mast-cell stabilizing vegetables (broccoli, asparagus, onions dense in natural quercetin) and clean complex carbohydrates (potatoes, wild rice).

### Phase 3: Rebuild & Scale
*   **Operational Focus:** Suppress systemic tissue inflammation entirely to allow enterocytes to divide and completely regrow the flattened microvilli brush border, restoring native DAO synthesis.
*   **Duration:** 30 to 60 Days.
*   **Supplement Stack:**
    *   **Pure L-Glutamine Powder:** 5g to 10g dissolved in room-temperature water upon waking and right before bed. Functions as direct cellular fuel for enterocyte remodeling.
    *   **Zinc Carnosine:** 75mg twice daily with meals to deliver chelated zinc directly to the epithelial lining for tight junction repair.
    *   **Serum-Derived Bovine Immunoglobulins (SBI):** (e.g., *MegaIgG2000*) to act as a biological sponge, neutralizing residual bacterial toxins and lipopolysaccharides (LPS) before they alert underlying dermal or gut immune arrays.
*   **Dietary Baseline:** Solidification of low-histamine maintenance. Safe fats (ghee, extra virgin olive oil, raw pine nuts, macadamia butter) are systematically introduced.
